# Passage 1.12.3 Page Plan

## Passage

`1.12.3`

## Key Places, Events, and Objects

- Pyrrhus knows he cannot match the Romans conventionally and prepares elephants against them.
- Alexander first acquires elephants among European rulers after defeating Porus and the Indian force.
- Later Hellenistic kings also hold elephants, especially Antigonus.
- Pyrrhus gains his elephants as spoils from Demetrius.
- The Romans are terrified when the elephants appear, imagining them to be creatures of another kind.

## Main Visual

The main panel is generated raster art of Pyrrhus' elephants advancing against a Roman line in southern Italy. The composition emphasizes both historical orientation and the passage's emotional claim: the Roman soldiers' disciplined terror at animals they have never encountered.

## Insets

- Scenic inset: generated raster art of Alexander's army viewing Indian elephants after the struggle with Porus, establishing the precedent Pausanias names.
- Provenance/orientation panel: local textured route panel tracing India and Porus to Alexander, Hellenistic royal elephant corps, Demetrius, Pyrrhus, and the Roman encounter.
- Source/sequence panel: deterministic local text panel summarizing the logic of the passage.

## Orientation Strategy

Use the main art to show the immediate Roman battlefield encounter, then use the lower panels to explain how elephants enter Pausanias' Greek historical chain before arriving in Pyrrhus' Italian war. The locator remains subordinate and textured, not the page's main visual identity.
