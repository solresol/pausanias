# Passage 1.13.2 page plan

- **Passage:** 1.13.2
- **Key places:** Upper Macedonia; Thessaly; Pherai; Larissa; the coastal cities to which Antigonus retreats.
- **Key events and objects:** Pyrrhus defeats Antigonus and his Gallic mercenaries; captured Celtic armour and shields are dedicated to Athena Itonia; the dedicatory inscription identifies the Molossian victor.
- **Main visual:** a rich, historically grounded battlefield panorama at the moment the Macedonian and Gallic line breaks, with Pyrrhus visible as the commanding Molossian king and the defeated army withdrawing toward the distant coastal plain.
- **Scenic inset:** an intimate temple-treasury study of Celtic shields and armour newly hung as trophies in Athena Itonia's sanctuary. Any exact inscription is added locally, not generated in the art.
- **Orientation inset:** a painterly raised-relief view of Thessaly and southern Macedonia, with no baked-in text; local overlays identify Larissa, Pherai, Upper Macedonia, the sanctuary between Pherai and Larissa, and Antigonus's retreat toward the coast.
- **Orientation strategy:** the battlefield establishes the historical action; the relief inset locates the territorial consequence; the trophy study makes Pausanias's surviving evidence visually concrete.
- **Approved style references:** `graphic_book/images/1/1/4.png` and `graphic_book/images/1/1/5.png` for parchment atlas grammar, rich raster art, hierarchy, and coffee-table finish.
- **Continuity reference:** `graphic_book/images/1/13/1.png` for the immediate Pyrrhus narrative sequence only.
- **Suitability:** warriors are fully clothed/armoured; no nudity, gore, severed bodies, or eroticized figures.
