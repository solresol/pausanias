# Passage 1.12.2 Page Plan

## Passage

`1.12.2`

## Key Places, Events, and Objects

- Pyrrhus resolves on the Italian campaign without delay.
- Warships are filled and merchant vessels prepared to transport cavalry and infantry.
- The crossing hides Pyrrhus from Roman view until he appears in battle.
- Pausanias cites obscure "Memoirs of Deeds" and says the account made him admire Pyrrhus' courage and foresight.
- Tarentum, the Ionian crossing, Rome, ships, horses, infantry, and battlefield surprise are the main orientation cues.

## Main Visual

The main panel is a generated raster historical scene of Pyrrhus directing warships and merchant transports at a fortified Greek harbor. Horses, infantry, stores, rigging, and ships make the logistics of the passage visible before local labels and callouts are added.

## Insets

- Scenic inset: generated raster battlefield view of Pyrrhus' sudden arrival disrupting the Roman line after the unseen crossing.
- Secondary locator: local textured route panel marking Epirus, the Ionian Sea, Tarentum, Rome, and the hidden route.
- Source/sequence panel: local deterministic text panel summarizing decision, transports, concealment, and surprise.

## Orientation Strategy

Use the main art to establish the material crossing preparations, then label Pyrrhus, warships, merchant transports, cavalry, and infantry/stores directly on the panel. The bottom locator gives geographic direction without becoming the page's main visual identity. The battlefield inset completes the passage's narrative sequence by showing why the unseen arrival mattered.
