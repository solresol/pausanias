# Passage 1.13.3 page plan

- **Passage:** 1.13.3
- **Key places:** Athena Itonia's sanctuary in Thessaly; Zeus's sanctuary at Dodona in Epirus; Macedonia; the gold-rich lands of Asia recalled by the dedicatory verse.
- **Key events and objects:** Gallic shields dedicated after Pyrrhus's victory over Antigonus; Macedonian shields consecrated to Zeus at Dodona; the inscription reverses Macedonia's former conquests into ownerless spoils; Cleonymus prevents Pyrrhus from completing Macedonia's subjugation.
- **Main visual:** a monumental, richly painted archaeological reconstruction at Dodona: captured Macedonian shields hung beside the weathered columns of Zeus's sanctuary, with the sacred oak and Epirote mountains establishing place. No generated writing appears on the stone or trophies.
- **Scenic inset:** Pyrrhus and the Spartan prince Cleonymus in a tense, fully clothed command encounter above a Macedonian valley, making visible the political turn that leaves conquest incomplete.
- **Orientation inset:** a painterly raised-relief view from Dodona and Epirus eastward into Macedonia and south-east toward Thessaly, without baked-in labels; local overlays identify the trophy sites and the theatre of conquest.
- **Orientation strategy:** the main panel establishes Dodona and the physical evidence; the relief inset connects the two sanctuaries to Macedonia; the command inset explains why the victory did not become total conquest.
