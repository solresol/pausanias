# Passage 1.13.6 Page Plan

- Passage ID: 1.13.6
- Key places/events/objects: Sparta and the Eurotas valley; Pyrrhus and the Epirote invasion; Spartan, Argive, and Messenian defenders; the near direct assault on the city; ravaged territory and booty; the earlier war with Demetrius; defensive trenches, deep moats, palisades, and fortified buildings.
- Main visual: a historically grounded panoramic reconstruction of Pyrrhus's assault on Sparta, viewed obliquely across the Eurotas valley. The defended edge of the unwalled city is visible behind a deep trench, timber palisade, and buildings incorporated into the defensive line, while disciplined Epirote infantry and elephants press toward the works. The scene must read immediately as a difficult assault on improvised urban fortifications, not as a generic battle.
- Insets: a generated textured relief view of Laconia and the Eurotas valley, locally labelled with Sparta, the Eurotas, Taygetus, and the northern approach; a close crop of the main art used as a defensive-works study, with deterministic labels for trench, palisade, and fortified houses; a small locally typeset sequence explaining the four hostile shocks named by Pausanias.
- Orientation strategy: the main-scene labels identify the city, Eurotas valley, defensive line, and Epirote assault; the relief locator fixes Sparta between Taygetus and Parnon and marks Pyrrhus's approach; callouts connect the assault directly to the fortifications prepared during the earlier war with Demetrius.
